Portfolio

Indian Steele Park Animal Buoy Project

A collaborative effort under ASU’s EPICS program to design a sustainable buoy system that aids wildlife, particularly ducks, in safely exiting the steep banks of the park’s pond.

Key Features

  • Stable and buoyant design to support multiple animals.
  • Eco-friendly materials: High-Density Polyethylene (HDP).
  • Easy installation and minimal maintenance.
  • Integrated ramp for safe animal access.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Challenge: Stability in varying water conditions.
    Solution: Adjustable anchoring mechanisms.
  • Challenge: Eco-friendly design.
    Solution: Used HDP for durability and environmental safety.

My Contributions

  • Led research and development of prototypes.
  • Managed team communication and timelines.
  • Coordinated with stakeholders to align project goals.

Outcome

A functional and scalable solution for wildlife accessibility in urban parks, combining sustainability and innovative engineering.
